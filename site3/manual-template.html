Technique: Autonomous Rover
Rank: Advanced
Category: Robotics
Author: SectLeader

Technique Overview

The Autonomous Rover is a wheeled robotic platform capable of navigating an environment without human input. Using an array of ultrasonic sensors, it detects and avoids obstacles in real time. This technique teaches motor control, sensor integration, and basic autonomous decision logic.

This is a flagship Advanced technique of the Iron Foundry Sect — expect to spend 2–4 weeks on the full build and tune cycle.

Artifacts Required

Gather these components before beginning cultivation. Substitutions are noted where applicable.

🔩
Arduino Uno R3 — or compatible microcontroller (Nano, Mega)
⚙
L298N Motor Driver Module — dual H-bridge for DC motor control
📡
HC-SR04 Ultrasonic Sensors (x3) — front, left, right placement
🔋
DC Gear Motors (x4) + Wheels — TT motors recommended for chassis
🔌
18650 Battery Pack (7.4V) — with XT60 connector and power switch
🗜️
Chassis Frame — acrylic or 3D printed platform, min 20cm x 15cm
📎
Jumper Wires, Breadboard, Zip Ties — standard wiring materials

Cultivation Steps

1
Assemble the chassis.

Mount the four gear motors to the corners of the chassis frame. Secure with M3 bolts. Attach wheels firmly. Ensure the frame is level and motors are symmetrically placed.

2
Wire the motor driver.

Connect motor pairs to OUT1/OUT2 and OUT3/OUT4 on the L298N. Connect ENA/ENB to PWM pins on Arduino (pins 5, 6). Connect IN1–IN4 to digital pins 7, 8, 9, 10.

3
Mount and wire the sensors.

Attach HC-SR04 sensors at the front, left-front, and right-front positions. Connect VCC to 5V, GND to GND, and TRIG/ECHO to digital pins 2/3, 4/5, 11/12 respectively.

4
Upload the firmware.

Clone the repository below. Open rover_main.ino in Arduino IDE. Select your board and COM port. Upload to the Arduino.

5
Power on and calibrate.

Connect the battery pack. Open Serial Monitor at 9600 baud to observe sensor readings. Adjust the OBSTACLE_DISTANCE constant if the rover detects too early or too late.

6
Test in open space first.

Place the rover on a flat surface with clear obstacles. Observe its avoidance behavior. Tune motor speeds in the code until the rover drives straight and turns consistently.

Outcome & Lessons Learned

After completing this technique, the rover successfully navigated a maze environment with an obstacle avoidance success rate of ~92%. Key findings:

  • Motor speed matching is critical — even slightly mismatched motors cause drift.
  • Ultrasonic sensors have a blind spot below 2cm — mount them at an angle.
  • A third rear sensor would prevent wall-collision during reverse maneuvers.
  • Next iteration: add encoders for odometry and closed-loop speed control.
